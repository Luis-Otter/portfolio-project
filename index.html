Signs/Symptoms

Understanding mental health is the first step toward caring for yourself and others. Just like your physical health, your mental health deserves attention and care.

Feeling nervous, restless, or tense frequently.

Persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, or a lack of interest in activities you once enjoyed.

Overwhelm from school, relationships, or expectations.

Avoiding friends, family, or social activities.

Difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep, or sleeping too much.

Coping Strategies


  • Talk About It

    Talking to a trusted friend, parent, teacher, or counselor can help. It may seem difficult, but opening up often provides relief and perspective.


  • Mindfulness

    Deep breathing exercises, guided meditations, or simply taking 5-10 minutes to relax and clear your mind can reduce stress and anxiety.


  • Exercise

    Physical activity boosts your mood by releasing endorphins. Whether it’s going for a walk, doing yoga, or playing a sport, find what works for you.


  • Routine

    Regular sleep patterns, balanced meals, and staying hydrated can improve your mood and energy levels.


  • Limit Social Media

    Social media can sometimes contribute to feelings of inadequacy or anxiety. Set limits on your screen time, and don’t compare your life to what you see online.

Where to Find Help

If you feel like you're struggling with your mental health or you’re concerned about a friend, there are resources available to you. Here’s where you can start:

  • School Counselor

    Most schools have counselors trained to help with mental health issues. They are a great first step in getting the support you need.

  • Local Therapists

    Find a therapist near you by searching online or asking your doctor for a recommendation.

FAQs:

  • What should I do if I’m feeling overwhelmed?

    Start by taking a deep breath and focusing on one task at a time. Reach out to someone you trust, and if the feeling persists, consider speaking with a counselor.


  • How do I tell my parents I need help with my mental health?

    Choose a time when you can talk calmly. Be honest about how you’re feeling and let them know that seeking professional help could be beneficial for you.


  • What if my friend doesn’t want help?

    You can encourage them, but ultimately it’s their choice to seek help. If you’re concerned about their safety, speak to a trusted adult immediately.
